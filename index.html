Doctrine I — Ø

Sound is architecture the body remembers.

MetalHolic is constructed as a three-day emotional architecture inside Red Square. Each day represents a measured shift in intensity — from atmosphere, through chaos, into reflection. This is not entertainment. It is documentation of emotional and spatial states through sound.

For its third edition the festival expands its archive into the monumental gravity of central Moscow. The granite is the stage. The crowd is the chorus. The history is the distortion already in the air.

Doctrine II

A dystopian archive of sound

The festival is documented as a public archive. Recordings, posters, transcripts and obituaries are filed under each edition and made permanent in the MetalHolic Library.

Doctrine III

No spectator, only witness

Capacity is limited and ticketed in three tiers. The crowd is treated as an instrument inside the spatial composition of the square — not as an audience.

Doctrine IV

Three days, one continuous breath

Performances are programmed without pauses between stages. Sound bleeds from one act into the next; silence is composed, never accidental.
