Policy

A plain-language explanation of how the MetalHolic Foundation processes personal data, in compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — "GDPR") and applicable national legislation.

1 — Data controller

The data controller, in the meaning of Article 4(7) GDPR, is the MetalHolic Foundation, a non-profit cultural foundation registered in the Russian Federation under registration number 7705012345, with registered office at Bolshoy Cherkasskiy lane, 11, 109012 Moscow.

2 — Data Protection Officer

The Foundation has appointed a Data Protection Officer ("DPO") in accordance with Article 37 GDPR. The DPO can be contacted at dpo@metalholic.fest or by post at the registered office, marked "DPO — confidential".

3 — What we process & why

3.1 Visitors of the website

IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, navigation patterns and page-load metrics. Used for security monitoring, abuse prevention and aggregated, anonymous analytics. No profiling, no advertising IDs, no third-party trackers.

3.2 Ticket buyers

Full name, e-mail, postal address (where applicable), date of birth, payment-card token (we never see your full card number), order history. Used to issue tickets, manage entry, prevent fraud and answer customer-support queries.

3.3 Forum members

Username, e-mail, hashed password, posts, IP address at registration and last login, optional avatar. Used to operate the discussion forum and enforce community rules.

3.4 Press & accreditation

Full name, outlet, professional e-mail, identity-document number for verification (deleted after the festival), accreditation history. Used to verify and grant press access.

3.5 Newsletter subscribers

E-mail address, language, opt-in timestamp. Used to send a maximum of six (6) editorial newsletters per year. You can unsubscribe in one click.

3.6 Contact-form senders

Name, e-mail, free-text message, topic. Used to answer your enquiry and to keep the case file. Not used for marketing.

4 — Legal bases

  • Article 6(1)(b) — performance of a contract: ticket purchase, accreditation, forum membership.
  • Article 6(1)(c) — legal obligation: tax records, accounting, security records.
  • Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interest: security, fraud prevention, aggregated analytics, replying to non-contractual messages.
  • Article 6(1)(a) — consent: newsletter, optional cookies, optional marketing.

6 — Recipients & transfers

Personal data is processed inside the Foundation by authorised staff on a need-to-know basis. We share data with the following categories of processors, under written agreements that comply with Article 28 GDPR:

  • EU-hosted cloud-infrastructure provider (website & databases);
  • EU-based payment-services provider (PSP) for ticket transactions;
  • EU-based e-mail-delivery provider for transactional and newsletter mail;
  • Independent accounting firm registered in the Russian Federation;
  • Law-enforcement or judicial authorities, only upon a valid legal request.

If, exceptionally, data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, such transfer is governed by Standard Contractual Clauses or another transfer mechanism recognised by the European Commission.

7 — Your rights

Under Articles 15 to 22 GDPR, you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • request rectification or erasure of inaccurate or out-of-date data;
  • restrict or object to processing in defined circumstances;
  • portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format;
  • withdraw consent at any time, without affecting prior processing;
  • not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing.

To exercise any of these rights, write to dpo@metalholic.fest. We respond within thirty (30) days, extended by a further sixty (60) days where strictly necessary, with reasons.

8 — Security measures

We protect personal data using industry-standard technical and organisational measures, including: encryption of data in transit and at rest where applicable, access control, role-based permissions, logging, regular security reviews, staff training, and a documented incident-response procedure.

9 — Minors

The website is not directed at children under 14. Ticket purchases by minors require an adult ticket-holder. The forum is open to users from 16 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from younger users; if you believe we have, please contact the DPO and we will delete it.

10 — Lodging a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular in the EU member state of your habitual residence, your place of work or the place of the alleged infringement. A list of European supervisory authorities is published by the European Data Protection Board.
