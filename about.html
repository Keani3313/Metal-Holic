MetalHolicwhat this festival actually is

Mi MetalHolic is not a press kit, not an about page and not a brand story. It is a personal text about what the festival represents — identity, emotion, expression, and the right to be loud and individual at the same time.

Manifesto · I

A festival for the loud part of growing up.

MetalHolic exists because the music that mattered most to a generation — nu-metal, alternative, post-hardcore — was treated as a phase, a costume, a marketing category. It wasn’t. It was an emotional language: how a teenager in 2001 said “I am angry, I am soft, I am alone, I am exactly who I am.”

This festival is a love letter to that language. To CD-Rs traded in school corridors, to band shirts kept long after they fit, to records that explained things home and school could not. It is built so that anyone who lived inside that vocabulary — or wants to discover it now — can stand in one square for three days and feel it as a continuous piece of music.

Each edition is one piece in three movements. Atmosphere opens slow and emotional — the bands that rehearsed loneliness as a craft. Chaos is the nu-metal core: the riff, the scream, the bounce, the bands the playlists forgot. Legacy connects everything to the records and people that came before — helmet, quicksand, the heavy alternative canon.

The schedule is composed without true silence between sets. The marquees move. Sound bleeds from one stage into the next. Three days, one breath — the festival is a single continuous piece performed by the city as much as by the bands.

Doctrine

Operating principles

★ I

No spectator

The crowd is treated as part of the composition. No barriers between performance and audience that aren't necessary for safety.

★ II

Limited capacity

32 000 tickets total. No expansion. No re-issue. The square sets the limit, not the spreadsheet.

★ III

Permanent archive

Every edition is documented and filed. The archive is open to attendees with a General Pass and to working press through accreditation.

★ IV

No phones, no spotlight

Recording is restricted. Phones are permitted but discouraged. The Main stage is not lit by sponsor branding.

★ V

Curation over volume

Volume is a tool, not a metric. Programming favours emotional precision over loudness records.

★ VI

The square is the stage

We do not build sets that try to overshadow the square. We use the architecture as it is and amplify it as it is.
