MetalHolic

Not a festival. A fictional cultural reconstruction. A monument that breathes for three days a year. An archive that refuses to behave like one.

Manifesto · I

We do not produce events.
We document conditions.

MetalHolic is constructed as a hybrid between festival, archive and museum exhibition. The Red Square edition is not a tourist programme — it is a fictional reconstruction of collective memory, written in the language of underground metal. Every artist on the bill is a witness; every member of the audience is an instrument inside a spatial composition.

The festival exists because Moscow's monumental architecture continues to broadcast its history regardless of who listens. We are not adding to that broadcast. We are choosing — for three days a year — to listen to it more carefully, and louder.

Each edition is built around a three-day emotional architecture. Atmosphere opens the descent: it is slow, immersive, layered. Chaos collapses structure: feedback, repetition, hardcore intensity. Legacy closes by returning the audience to the historical body of the genre — drone, doom, post-metal, ritual.

The schedule is composed without true silence between sets. The marquees move. The sound bleeds. The square breathes. The festival is one continuous piece in three movements, performed by the city as much as by the artists.

Manifesto · II

Three days, one breath.

Doctrine

Operating principles

★ I

No spectator

The crowd is treated as part of the composition. No barriers between performance and audience that aren't necessary for safety.

★ II

Limited capacity

32 000 tickets total. No expansion. No re-issue. The square sets the limit, not the spreadsheet.

★ III

Permanent archive

Every edition is documented and filed. The archive is open to attendees with a General Pass and to working press through accreditation.

★ IV

No phones, no spotlight

Recording is restricted. Phones are permitted but discouraged. The Main stage is not lit by sponsor branding.

★ V

Curation over volume

Volume is a tool, not a metric. Programming favours emotional precision over loudness records.

★ VI

The square is the stage

We do not build sets that try to overshadow the square. We use the architecture as it is and amplify it as it is.
